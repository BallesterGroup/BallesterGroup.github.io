Biography

I graduated from M.Sc. Machine Learning at UCL, and I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Jun Wang, and have also closely worked with Dr. Tommaso Pasini on my master’s research thesis for cross-modal music generation. I completed my B.S. in Computer Science and B.S. in Statistics with double minors in Mathematics and Information Systems and Statistical Analysis at Penn State University.

My research motivations lie in Artificial Intelligence for Science & Arts, and sepcifically in multimodal learning applied to 3D/4D modeling and generation. Now I am interested in AI algorithms for Strucute-based Drug Development. I used to work on multimodal machine learning for drug discovery and generative arts, as well as applied deep reinforcement learning in energy management and autonomous driving.

    • Research Projects

    Lyric-to-melody Generation with Two-Stage Modelling
    We proposed a two-stage modeling approach that utilize pitch as a bridge to link the modality difference between lyric and melody. Both of the subjective results and objective results from the experiment indicate our method is able to be competent in generating harmonious and natural melodies from given lyrics.
    An Empirical study on Sentence Classification with BERT-series Contextual Embedding
    We studied a more effective method of BERT-series contextual pre-trained language model in the task of sentence classification.
    Multitask Learning for Image Recognition, Segmentation, and Classification
    We conducted a multi-task learning system with cross-stitch soft-parameter sharing architecture based on ResNet for Image Recognition, Segmentation, and Classification, and show such nueral network structure improves the performance of every single task model.
    Leveraging Deep RL in Smart Grids
    Through experiments of various Deep RL algorithms, for example PPO, SAC, and DDPG, in the Simulink, we demonstrated efficient application of such approaches on instances involving the smart grids
    Curiosity-driven Exploration for Motion Planning
    We explored a DQN-based deep reinforcement learning algorithm for motion planning of autonomous driving. The idea is to train agent with intrinsic curiosity-based motivation when external rewards from environment are sparse.
